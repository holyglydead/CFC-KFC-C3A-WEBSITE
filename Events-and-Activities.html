MONTHLY CHAPTER ASSEMBLY

The Monthly Chapter Assembly of Kids for Christ (KFC) is a regular gathering that focuses on nurturing childrens faith, fostering friendships, and promoting fun in a Christ-centered environment. Each assembly revolves around a specific theme, reflecting the core values of KFC and its mission to form young children in the Catholic faith.

Key Components

1. Opening Worship
The assembly begins with lively praise and worship songs to create a joyful and welcoming atmosphere. Simple prayers and actions help children engage with God in a fun and meaningful way.

2. Theme-Based Teaching
A Bible-based lesson or story, presented in a creative and interactive format like skits, storytelling, or videos. The teaching focuses on virtues, the life of Christ, or Catholic teachings that are relatable to children.

3. Fun Activities
Games, arts and crafts, or team-building exercises that reinforce the theme of the day. These activities encourage creativity, collaboration, and excitement.

4. Group Sharing and Reflection
Children are encouraged to share their thoughts and experiences related to the lesson. Facilitators guide reflections to help children understand how to apply the teachings in their daily lives.

5. Closing Prayer and Commitment
The assembly concludes with a prayer of thanksgiving and a commitment to practice the lessons learned.



Benefits

Faith Formation: Children grow closer to God and learn Catholic values in a fun and engaging way. Community Building: The assembly strengthens bonds among children and their families within the KFC community.

Skill Development: Through activities, kids develop social skills, teamwork, and self-expression.


The Monthly Chapter Assembly is not only a time of learning but also a celebration of faith, friendship, and fun, leaving a lasting impact on the spiritual journey of every child involved.

JUNIOR KIDS DAY

Junior Kids Day is a special event within Kids for Christ (KFC) designed specifically for younger members, typically aged 4 to 8. It focuses on introducing the foundational aspects of the Christian faith in a fun, interactive, and age-appropriate way. The event is designed to engage children while helping them grow spiritually and emotionally in a Christ-centered environment.

Key Components

1. Opening Worship
The day begins with lively, easy-to-follow songs and prayers to introduce the theme and set a joyful tone.

2. Bible Stories and Teaching
Stories from the Bible are presented in a creative and engaging format, such as through puppetry, animated videos, or dramatized storytelling.

3. Fun and Interactive Activities
Games, crafts, and hands-on activities that tie into the theme of the day. These activities are designed to reinforce the lessons learned and encourage creativity and teamwork.

4. Group Discussions
Age-appropriate group discussions or sharing sessions where children are encouraged to reflect on the stories and lessons of the day.

5. Closing Celebration
The day ends with a closing prayer and perhaps a small celebration where children are recognized for their participation.



Benefits

Faith Development: Children learn the basics of the Catholic faith in a joyful and accessible way.

Social and Emotional Growth: They develop friendships and learn important social skills through group activities.

Creative Expression: Through crafts, games, and songs, children have an outlet to express their creativity while reinforcing Christian teachings.



Junior Kids Day is a perfect event to introduce younger children to the concepts of faith, community, and love for God while allowing them to have fun and create lasting memories.

SENIOR KIDS CAMP

The Senior Kids Camp is a special event within Kids for Christ (KFC) designed for older children, typically aged 9 to 12. It is a spiritually enriching camp experience that combines fun, learning, and personal growth. The camp aims to deepen the children’s faith, build leadership skills, and strengthen their relationships with God, while also fostering a sense of community with other kids in the program.

Key Components

1. Spiritual Formation
The camp begins and ends each day with vibrant worship sessions that help children connect with God through songs, prayers, and reflections. It’s a time for deepening their spiritual life and cultivating a love for prayer.

2. Outdoor Activities and Team-building
Through group activities, the children learn to work together, communicate effectively, and build lasting friendships. These exercises also help them grow in patience, trust, and understanding.

3. Leadership Training
Senior Kids Camp includes leadership-building activities where older children can learn important skills like organizing, facilitating, and guiding others. Workshops may also cover topics such as responsibility, decision-making, and serving others.

4. Fun and Creative Activities
The camp includes creative workshops, such as arts and crafts, where children can express themselves artistically while reflecting on the camp’s themes.

5. Community and Fellowship
Children have the opportunity to share their experiences and reflect on the lessons they've learned throughout the camp. Facilitators guide discussions to help kids understand the importance of Christian fellowship and how to live out their faith.

6. Closing Ceremony
The camp typically ends with a closing ceremony or celebration where children are recognized for their participation and accomplishments. It may include a final group prayer, worship, and a reflection on the growth they experienced during the camp.



Benefits

Spiritual Growth: The camp deepens children's faith, helping them understand their relationship with God and how to live as Christians in their daily lives.

Leadership Development: Children gain valuable leadership skills that prepare them to take on roles in KFC and other areas of their lives. w

Character Building: Through team-building activities, children grow in virtues like responsibility, perseverance, and kindness.

Community Building: The camp strengthens bonds among children, fostering a sense of belonging and unity in the KFC community.



The Senior Kids Camp is an empowering, fun, and spiritually enriching experience that equips older children with the tools they need to live out their faith, take on leadership roles, and build meaningful relationships with God and others.

GLOBAL DAY OF SERVICE

The Global Day of Service is an annual event within Kids for Christ (KFC) where children and families around the world come together to engage in acts of service and outreach. It is designed to instill in children the values of compassion, generosity, and social responsibility, encouraging them to live out their faith by helping others in practical ways.

Key Components

1. Acts of Service
Children participate in various service projects such as distributing food, cleaning up local environments, visiting the sick or elderly, or helping in shelters. These activities are aimed at making a positive impact on the local community.

2. Spiritual Reflection
Throughout the day, children learn stories and teachings from the Bible that emphasize serving others as an important aspect of Christian life (e.g., the Good Samaritan, Jesus washing the feet of His disciples).

3. Teamwork and Fellowship
The Global Day of Service encourages collaboration and teamwork. Children work in groups to complete different service projects, allowing them to bond with peers and develop a sense of unity.

4. Creativity and Innovation
Some chapters may incorporate creative service activities, such as making cards for hospital patients, creating care packages, or crafting items for charity. These activities allow children to use their skills and talents to serve others.

5. Global Unity
The Global Day of Service is celebrated simultaneously in different parts of the world, with KFC chapters from various countries coming together to serve their communities. This global participation emphasizes the universal call to serve and unite as one global KFC family. Through this event, children learn about the broader impact of serving others, and some projects may even extend to global initiatives, such as supporting international missions or disaster relief efforts.



Benefits

Spiritual Growth: Children learn that service is a powerful way to live out their faith and follow Christ’s example.

Development of Empathy: The experience fosters empathy, compassion, and a greater understanding of the needs of others.

Community Engagement: It provides an opportunity for children to actively contribute to their communities, making a tangible difference.

Sense of Global Community: The event fosters a sense of belonging to a larger global community of KFC members who are united in their mission to serve others.



The Global Day of Service is not only a time for children to serve, but it also helps them internalize the Christian value of service as a lifelong commitment. It's an opportunity to make a positive impact in the world while growing in faith and love for others.

ROCK FACILITATORS WEEKEND TRAINING

The ROCK Facilitators Weekend Training is an event within Kids for Christ (KFC) aimed at training and equipping young leaders, often older members of the KFC community, to become effective facilitators in leading ROCK (Reaching Out Christ to Kids) activities. This program is specifically designed to empower participants with the skills and knowledge they need to guide and mentor younger children in the KFC program, helping them to develop strong leadership abilities in a Christian context.

Key Components

1. Leadership Development
The training equips participants with key skills for facilitating activities, Bible lessons, and group discussions. They learn how to engage children effectively, manage groups, and lead with confidence.

2. Spiritual Formation
Facilitators undergo spiritual formation through sessions that help them strengthen their personal relationship with God. This helps them lead from a place of genuine faith and commitment to Christ.

3. Skill-building Workshops
The weekend includes workshops that focus on practical aspects of leadership, such as organizing activities, managing groups, and fostering a positive, supportive environment for kids.

4. Team-building
The training includes team-building exercises where participants work together on activities and challenges. These exercises are designed to help them build trust, learn how to delegate tasks, and foster a spirit of unity.

5. Hands-On Practice
The training includes simulated activities or mock sessions where participants get a chance to practice what they’ve learned in a real-life setting, with feedback from mentors and peers.

6. Reflection and Commitment
The weekend concludes with a commitment ceremony or reflection, where participants pledge to serve faithfully as facilitators, making a personal commitment to help other kids grow in their faith.



Benefits

Empowered Leadership: The training helps participants become confident, capable leaders who can inspire and guide younger children in their spiritual journey.

Faith Growth: Facilitators deepen their personal faith and understanding of God's Word, strengthening their ability to pass on that knowledge and love to others.

Community Building: The training fosters a sense of belonging and community among future leaders, building strong relationships that support each other’s growth.

Effective Mentorship: Participants learn how to be effective mentors and role models for younger children, helping them grow in faith and character.



Junior Kids Day is a perfect event to introduce younger children to the concepts of faith, community, and love for God while allowing them to have fun and create lasting memories.
